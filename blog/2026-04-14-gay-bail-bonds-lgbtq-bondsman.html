How a Southern California Bail Bond Actually Works

You call (626) 478-1062. We confirm the bail amount from the applicable county bail schedule — gay bail bonds typically range from $5,000 for petty theft (PC 484) to $50,000 for domestic violence (PC 273.5) and $100,000 for DUI with injury (VC 23153). You pay Angels Bail Bonds a 10% premium — the state-set rate under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. We post the full bail amount with the jail as a surety bond. The defendant is released, typically 1–4 hours at nearest jail and 4–8 hours at the applicable county Inmate Reception Center.

The 10% is a fee for services — it is a premium paid to the bondsman, not a loan. When the case concludes and all court appearances are made, the bond is exonerated.

How much does a bail bond actually cost in Southern California?

The bail bond fee is 10% of the total bail amount under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. For a first-offense DUI with bail set at $15,000, you pay $1,500. That 10% is our fee for posting the full bond — it is not refunded at case conclusion. Payment plans are available for qualifying clients.

Can Southern California bail be posted the same night?

Yes, in most cases. California does not impose a mandatory overnight hold.
