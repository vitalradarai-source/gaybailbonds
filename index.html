How Do Bail Bonds Work in California?

When someone is arrested, a judge sets a bail amount based on the charges, criminal history, and flight risk. Most families cannot pay the full amount in cash.

You pay Angels Bail Bonds 10% of the total bail amount (regulated by California Insurance Code § 1800.4). We post the full bond and your loved one is released. The 10% is our fee — not a deposit.

LGBTQ+ Bail Bonds — Your Questions, Answered Respectfully

From a licensed California bail bondsman. Private, judgment-free, 24/7.

Can a chosen-family member or same-sex partner co-sign a bail bond?

Yes. California bail indemnification is based on financial capacity and willingness to take responsibility — not biological relationship or marriage. A partner, chosen-family member, friend, or roommate with acceptable credit or collateral can co-sign. We do not require proof of marriage, biological family, or legal kinship of any kind.

How do you handle gender marker and name concerns at booking?

We work with the booking information the jail provides and use your loved one’s current name and pronouns in every conversation you have with us. California sheriff booking records use legal name at arrest, which is outside our control, but our paperwork, communication, and approach with your family respects your loved one’s identity. Discretion is standard, not a request you have to make.

Is this call confidential? Will employers or family find out?

Your call to us is confidential. Bail bond records are court records and may become public through the court system, but we do not notify employers, family, neighbors, landlords, or anyone else. Our agents do not discuss your case outside the scope of posting the bond. Payment can be arranged privately without mailed statements to a shared address if needed.

How much does a California bail bond cost and what if no one has 10%?

California law (Insurance Code § 1800.4) sets the bail bond premium at 10% of the full bail — non-refundable, paid once. For families who cannot pay 10% up front, we offer zero-down options and payment plans based on combined household credit, collateral (vehicle title, home equity), or a qualified co-signer. We review options in one call — no credit check just to ask.
